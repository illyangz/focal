Timeline

Tracks: video (cuts appear on it in red) · audio waveform · zoom · camera · overlays · masks. Drag blocks to move, drag edges to resize, double-click to remove, click to edit/select. Darkened ends = trimmed off.

Overlays

Slides fill the frame with text; annotations are draggable labels; video clips overlay the recording (B-roll, intros). Images: drag on the preview to position, resize with the slider above — click a block to select it first. A watermark image stays on screen for the whole video, ignoring its timeline trim. All added at the playhead.

Masking i

Masks pixelate sensitive areas and track your zooms. Click a gray block to select a mask, then drag the patch on the preview to place it.

Cursor i

The system cursor baked into the capture is erased each frame (a soft blur at its exact logged position), then re-drawn as vector art — sharp at any size, smoothed, fading when idle, and able to glide home for loopable videos.

How Focal works

Getting Started

  • Pick Entire screen or a specific window from the grid.
  • For a screen, you can optionally turn on Record a custom area and drag out a region — this must be set before you hit record, not after.
  • Add a Webcam bubble or Microphone narration if you want them.
  • Hit Start Recording — after a 3-second countdown, Focal minimizes and recording begins. Clicks are tracked automatically for zoom.
  • Stop with the Stop Recording button, or press ⌘⇧S (Ctrl+Shift+S) from any app.

Auto Zoom & Cursor

  • Focal detects your clicks during recording and automatically creates zoom-in moments around them — no manual keyframing needed.
  • In the editor, purple blocks on the timeline are these zoom moments. Drag to move, drag the edges to resize, double-click to remove, or double-click the preview to re-aim one.
  • For window recordings, the real system cursor is excluded from the capture and replaced with a smooth vector cursor, redrawn from tracked mouse movement.

Editing

  • Timeline: trim the start/end, or cut out sections entirely (they're skipped on export).
  • Overlays: add text slides, draggable annotations, images, or B-roll clips at the playhead.
  • Masking: pixelate sensitive info — the mask follows your zooms automatically.
  • Canvas / Background / Layout: pick an aspect ratio, background, padding, corner radius, and shadow for the framed output.

Export

  • Choose a format and resolution, optionally enhance the voice track, then hit Export Video.
  • Use Copy shareable link afterward to upload and get a link (~30-day retention).
